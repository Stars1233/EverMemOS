# Developer Company as Key Hub

## When to use
When the question describes the game's development company indirectly — through the meaning of its name (e.g., "named after an amphibian"), founder characteristics, company history, or associated foundation activities.

## Technique
Game questions often identify games through their development company. Decode metaphorical descriptions ("named after an amphibian" → frog/toad), search for foundation activities, or look up founder backgrounds. Once the company is identified, search its game catalog to find the target game.

## Query Templates
- `software company named after [animal/meaning] [specific term]`
- `gaming company foundation established schools [countries]`
- `[company name] games catalog [era] [platform]`

## Worked Examples

### Example
- Question: Game by a company named after an amphibian, had a different name in early 1990s
- Search: `software company named after amphibian frog toad`
- Found: Albino Frog Software
- Verification: `Albino Frog Software Night Sky game DOS shareware 1992 1993`
- Why it worked: Decoded "amphibian" → frog/toad and found the company directly

### Example
- Question: Company whose foundation built schools in two countries
- Search: `gaming company foundation established schools two countries 1998 2006`
- Found: Smilegate Foundation

## Anti-pattern
- **Ignoring company name meanings**: Searching `game company amphibian` literally instead of expanding to `frog toad` misses the answer. Always decode metaphorical descriptions.
