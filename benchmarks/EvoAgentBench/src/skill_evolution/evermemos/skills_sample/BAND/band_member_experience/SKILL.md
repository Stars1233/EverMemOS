# Band: Member Experience Anchoring

## When to use
When a band question describes distinctive personal experiences of members rather than the band's discography.

## Technique
Members' unique life events are more searchable than band descriptions because they are rarer. Approach:
1. Identify the most unusual member experience described in the question ("triple bypass surgery", "moved to Finland", "voice acting career").
2. Search for the experience directly: `"musician triple bypass surgery"` or `"band member moved to Finland wife degree"`.
3. The search will likely return the member's name → then search `"[member name] band"` to find the band.
4. Verify band-level constraints (founding year, album count, style) against the candidate band.

This member-first approach is especially effective when the band itself is obscure but the member had a notable personal event.

## Query Templates
- `"musician [special event] [band keyword]"`
- `"[member name] Wikipedia band [band name]"`
- `"[member name] [band A] [band B] [band C] bands"`

## Worked Examples
**Mercyful Fate:** Question mentioned a member who had triple bypass surgery and was in bands with a body organ and a flower in their names. Searched `"musician triple bypass surgery"` → found King Diamond → `"King Diamond Mercyful Fate Brats Black Rose Brainstorm bands"` confirmed the answer.

**Rikki Ililonga / WITCH:** After identifying WITCH, verified the founding member with `"Rikki Ililonga Finland wife Sandi degree university"` — his relocation to Finland was the confirming detail.

## Anti-pattern
Only searching for the band name or discography while ignoring member clues. When a question describes a member's surgery, relocation, or unique side project, search for that experience first.
