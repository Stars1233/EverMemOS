# Band: Cultural Breadth

## When to use
When a band question suggests a non-Western music scene — African rock, Turkish rock, or other non-mainstream contexts.

## Technique
The band domain includes Zamrock (WITCH), Turkish rock (Duman), Ugandan jazz (Afrigo Band). Recognizing these contexts early prevents wasted searches.

Indicators of non-Western scenes:
- "Debut album recorded in a single day" — common in African/developing country contexts
- "Celebrated 40 years" with large member counts (8+) — suggests African big band tradition
- Geographic clues pointing to non-Western countries
- Independent label reissues (Now-Again Records specializes in reissuing African/Asian music)

Important: Non-Western bands may not be on Discogs or AllMusic. Their information may primarily exist on Wikipedia, regional music sites, or label pages. Adjust your search strategy accordingly.

## Query Templates
- `"[band name] [country] band [era]"`
- `"[record label] [band name] reissue [year]"`
- `""[N] years" [year] concert band formed [founding year] [member count] musicians"`

## Worked Examples
**Afrigo Band:** "Celebrated 40 years of music in 2015, composed of eight musicians." Searched `""40 years" 2015 concert band formed 1975 eight musicians"` → found Afrigo Band from Uganda's jazz scene.

**WITCH:** "Debut album recorded in a single day, released in the 1970s." Recognizing this as potentially Zamrock, searched `"WITCH Zambia band debut album recorded one day 1970s"` → confirmed the band from Zambia's rock scene.

## Anti-pattern
Thinking only of British and American bands. When clues mention unusual recording circumstances, large ensembles, or geographic indicators outside the West, actively consider African, Asian, and Middle Eastern music scenes.
